  Directions

By Train

There are regular services from London Paddington, London Marylebone and Birmingham to Oxford station. The service from London Marylebone also stops at Oxford Parkway. You can book with National Rail or Trainline.

From Oxford station: You can take the S3 and alight at the Radcliffe Observatory Quarter bus stop right outside the Mathematical Institute. Alternatively, you can take the 500 bus service and alight at the Keble Road bus stop which is 2 min walk away from the Mathematical Institute.

The walk from the Oxford station to the Mathematical Institute takes about 20 min.



From Oxford Parkway station: You can take the 500 bus service and alight at the Keble Road bus stop which is 2 min walk away from the Mathematical Institute.

By Coach

There are two 24-hour coach services departing from London Victoria and other London locations to Gloucester Green coach station in Oxford, the Oxford Tube and X90.

From Gloucester Green: You can take the S2 or S3 and alight at the Radcliffe Observatory Quarter bus stop right outside the Mathematical Institute. You can also take the 300 bus service to the Raddicliffe Observatory Quarter from the nearby George Street bus stop.

The walk from the Gloucester Green to the Mathematical Institute takes about 15 min.
