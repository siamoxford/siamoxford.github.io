Travel  

Getting to Oxford: We advise looking at trains or buses for getting to Oxford. The Mathematical Institute is less than a mile from both the Oxford Train Station and Gloucester Green Bus Station, so is accessible by foot or bike.

Taxis are readily available from both Oxford Train Station and Gloucester Green Coach Station and the fare to the Mathematical Institute should be approximately Â£7.00. Note there are a large number of external cycle racks located on the south and west sides of the building for those coming by bike.

Rail

There are regular services from London Paddington, London Marylebone and Birmingham to Oxford station. The service from London Marylebone also stops at Oxford Parkway. You can book with National Rail or Trainline.

Coach

There are two 24-hour coach services departing from London Victoria and other London locations to Gloucester Green coach station in Oxford, the Oxford Tube and X90.

See the National Express website for details of coaches from other major cities. Tickets can usually be purchases on the linked travel company websites.

Air

Travellers from abroad may arrive by plane at:
  • London Heathrow; the Oxford Bus Company Airline service coach goes directly from here to Oxford.
  • London Gatwick; the Oxford Bus Company Airline service coach goes directly from here to Oxford.
  • London Stansted; there are infrequent direct coaches or more frequent links via a coach to Heathrow or London.
  • Luton; you may need to take a coach to Heathrow or London to join one of the other services.
  • Birmingham International Airport; there are frequent trains from here to Oxford (see Rail Timetable Information).
